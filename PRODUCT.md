# Product

## Register

product

## Users

The primary user is a journal editor operating the tool alone on a desktop computer. They already understand ScholarOne and need a fast, reliable way to scan submissions, review generated reports, and execute carefully controlled rejection actions.

## Product Purpose

ScholarOne Reject Helper reduces repetitive editorial work while keeping irreversible actions understandable and deliberate. Success means the editor can configure a run, perform a safe dry run, understand its report, and start a rejection job without uncertainty about the selected report, limits, or current job state.

## Brand Personality

Technical, calm, and direct. The interface should feel like a dependable local utility: compact enough for regular use, but explicit whenever an action can affect manuscripts.

## Anti-references

Avoid colorful analytics dashboards, decorative SaaS cards, marketing-style hero sections, excessive animation, hidden controls, and visual effects that compete with the operational workflow. Do not make destructive actions look casual or equivalent to ordinary settings changes.

## Design Principles

1. Preserve the working automation flow and make its state easier to understand.
2. Lead with the safe workflow: configure, dry run, review, then act.
3. Keep technical detail available without forcing it into the primary path.
4. Make irreversible actions visually distinct and context-rich.
5. Prefer familiar controls, concise copy, and predictable keyboard behavior.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.2 AA where practical. Maintain visible keyboard focus, semantic controls, readable contrast, status announcements, and layouts that remain usable at narrow viewport widths. Avoid relying on color alone to communicate selection, errors, or destructive state.
